# Unit — Mental Models

Selected mental models from [mmpractices.com](https://mmpractices.com/) that fit product and UX decisions for Unit. One short paragraph per model and how it applies.

---

## Identity Change

- **Model**: Lasting change is easier when you shift identity (“I’m not a smoker”) rather than only behavior (“I’m trying to quit”). Identity drives habits.
- **Unit**: We reinforce “I’m someone who logs” through consistent, low-friction logging and clear completion (set done, session done). No guilt; just repeated small wins that support the identity. Optional overall feeling (1–5) supports reflection without making identity contingent on mood.

---

## Process over goals

- **Model**: Focusing on process (e.g. “I show up and log”) increases consistency and reduces burnout compared to outcome-only goals (“I must hit X kg”).
- **Unit**: The product is process-oriented: templates and sessions are about “doing the workout and logging it.” We don’t push goal weights in the core flow; we make the process (log sets, run rest, finish session) smooth. Goals can live in the user’s head or a future feature.

---

## Simplicity

- **Model**: Reducing complexity increases understandability, success rate, and adoption.
- **Unit**: Minimal surface—no social, no videos, no discovery. One job: log your program fast. Simplicity is the differentiator vs. bloated trackers and messy notes.

---

## Next Step

- **Model**: Progress comes from taking one more step; make the next step obvious and easy.
- **Unit**: “Next step” is always clear: “Complete this set,” “Start rest,” “Next exercise.” Defaults and one-tap reduce the cost of that next step. No ambiguity about what to do next in a workout.

---

## Breakdown Goals

- **Model**: Breaking goals into smaller pieces makes progress tangible and increases likelihood of continuation.
- **Unit**: A workout is “session → exercises → sets.” Each set is a small goal. Completing a set is a micro-win; completing a session is a bigger win. We don’t overload with long-term goals in v1—we make the immediate next step (this set) trivial to complete.

---

## High Agency

- **Model**: High-agency people look for ways to overcome a problem rather than accepting limits.
- **Unit**: Users who bring their own program are high-agency. We serve them by not imposing our program (no AI plans, no discovery). We give structure (templates, schema) and speed (Gym Test) so they can execute their plan. The product assumes the user is in charge.

---

## First Principles

- **Model**: Reaching the core reason for something leads to better solutions.
- **Unit**: First principle = “User has a program and needs to log sets quickly under physical stress.” Everything (defaults, one-tap, rest timer, no social) derives from that. We don’t add features that don’t serve this.

---

## Summary

| Model | Unit application |
|-------|------------------------|
| Identity Change | Reinforce “I log” via easy completion and history. |
| Process | Optimize for “log this set / this session,” not outcome goals in v1. |
| Simplicity | One job: fast, structured logging; no clutter. |
| Next Step | Always clear: complete set, start rest, next exercise. |
| Breakdown Goals | Set = micro-win; session = bigger win. |
| High Agency | User owns program; we provide structure and speed. |
| First Principles | “Log sets fast under stress” drives all design. |

These models guide prioritization and what we say no to (social, videos, required RPE, multi-step set entry).
